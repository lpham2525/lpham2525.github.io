Biography

I was born in Vietnam, and immigrated to the United States at the age of three. As a child, I had many dreams for what I wanted to be when I grew up, including children's book writer, zoologist, counselor, and working in the gift wrapping department of a store. I also enjoy mopping floors, for whatever reason. In college, I studied psychology, and later, social work. I used to work as a medical social worker but recently quit to learn coding. In my spare time, I love reading pop psychology books, baking square-shaped buttermilk biscuits, and drawing anime characters. I am also a fan of board games, foreign films, and Welsh corgis. Contact me if you'd like to know more!

Name Origin Story

When I was born, my parents took me to a fortune-teller who prophesized three things about my life:

  1. I would become a rough-and-tumble girl. A tomboy, in other words.
  2. I would be rich early on in life.
  3. I would become a dentist.
My parents were agreeable with these last two parts, but my father wanted to counteract the first prophecy. To this end, he named me LanChi, which means "orchid branch", because the orchid is supposed to be a delicate flower. Lo and behold, only the first prophecy has come true, proving the fortune-teller wrong and leaving my parents disappointed. Interestingly, there is a dentist in California who is also named LanChi Pham. Hmm...
